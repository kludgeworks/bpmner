# New employee onboarding

HR receives a signed offer letter from a new hire and files it in the HRIS.
Filing the offer letter kicks off three independent preparation tracks that
run in parallel.

The IT track provisions the employee's digital identity: it creates the
email account, configures single sign-on, issues a laptop, and grants
access to the team's tools and code repositories. The Facilities track
prepares the physical workspace: it assigns a desk, programs a building
access card, and stocks the desk with basic supplies. The Manager track
handles the social side: the hiring manager schedules orientation sessions,
assigns an onboarding buddy from the team, and adds the new hire to the
relevant team meetings and chat channels.

None of these three tracks depend on each other and they typically all
complete within two business days. The new hire's start date cannot be
confirmed until all three tracks have signalled completion — IT cannot send
the welcome email without the laptop being ready, Facilities cannot finish
without the building access card being active, and the manager cannot
finalise the orientation schedule without IT and Facilities done.

Once all three tracks are complete, the employee receives a welcome email
containing their day-one instructions, the building address, their
onboarding buddy's contact details, and links to the HR handbook. The
onboarding process is considered complete when the new hire confirms
receipt of the welcome email and that their first-day instructions are
clear.
