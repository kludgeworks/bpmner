# Customer refund request

When a customer submits a refund request through the support portal, the
request moves through three teams within the company before resolution.

The customer-support team picks up the request first. A support agent
reads the customer's description, confirms the order details, and
classifies the request as either "policy-eligible" (clear refund grounds
under the published return policy) or "judgement call" (needs review
beyond the policy text). Policy-eligible requests are approved on the
spot; judgement-call requests are routed onward.

The judgement-call requests land with the operations team. An operations
lead reviews the order history, the customer's tenure, and any prior
refund pattern. They either approve the refund with a written rationale
or escalate to finance with their recommendation.

The finance team handles the actual money movement. A finance clerk
verifies the refund amount against the original payment method, executes
the refund through the payment gateway, and notifies the customer that
the funds are on their way. Escalations from operations receive the
same finance-side treatment plus a brief manager review before
execution.

Once the refund is executed, the customer-support agent who originally
took the request closes the ticket and sends a follow-up confirmation
to the customer.
